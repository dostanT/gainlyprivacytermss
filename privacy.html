1. Overview

Gainly is a fitness progress tracking app for iOS. It lets you log workouts, track body measurements, photograph your physique, and receive AI-powered analysis of your progress over time.

This policy explains what data Gainly collects, why it collects it, where it is stored, and what rights you have. By using the app, you agree to the practices described here.

2. Data You Provide

2.1 Profile Information

  • Name (optional) — used to personalise greetings and reminders
  • Birth year — used to contextualise AI analysis and age-appropriate feedback
  • Gender — used for personalised fitness insights and cycle-aware training (if applicable)
  • Body measurements — weight, height, and optional circumference measurements you enter
  • Fitness goal — the training phase (Cut, Bulk, or Maintain) you select
  • Equipment profile — the gym equipment available to you

2.2 Activity Data

  • Progress photos — photos you take or import for visual progress tracking and AI analysis
  • Workout sessions — exercises, sets, reps, weights, and RPE ratings you log
  • Nutrition logs — calorie and macro entries you record
  • Daily check-ins — subjective wellbeing ratings you submit
  • Water intake — hydration logs you enter

2.3 What We Do NOT Collect

  • We do not require or collect your email address
  • We do not create user accounts
  • We do not collect precise GPS location
  • We do not track you across other apps or websites
  • We do not sell your data to third parties

3. Automatically Generated Data

  • Anonymous device identifier — a randomly generated UUID created on first launch, used solely to enforce usage limits. It is never linked to your name, Apple ID, or any personal identifier.
  • AI analysis counter — the number of analyses you have used this week (Pro) or in total (Free), stored server-side to enforce tier limits
  • App settings — your unit preferences, theme, notification schedule, and feature toggles, stored locally on your device

5. AI Analysis and Third-Party Processing

When you request an AI analysis, your photo and relevant body data are transmitted through our secure Firebase Cloud Functions proxy (hosted on Google Cloud, us-central1) to Anthropic, Inc. — the provider of the Claude AI model — for processing.

  • The proxy does not store your photos. Data is forwarded in memory and never written to disk or a database.
  • Anthropic processes the data solely to generate your analysis result.
  • We do not use your photos or personal data to train AI models.
  • Anthropic's handling of data is governed by their Privacy Policy and Usage Policy.

6. Data Storage

On Your Device

  • All workout sessions, nutrition logs, check-ins, body measurements, and app settings are stored locally using Apple's CoreData framework.
  • Progress photos are stored in your device's file system and are not uploaded to any server.
  • If iCloud is enabled, your data syncs across your Apple devices via iCloud CloudKit, governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

On Our Servers (Firebase / Firestore)

To enforce usage limits, we store the following minimal data in Google Firestore:

  • Anonymous device UUID — never linked to personal identity
  • AI analysis usage counter (free tier: lifetime total; Pro tier: weekly count and week start date)

No photos, health data, workout logs, or personally identifiable information are stored in Firestore.

Device Integrity (App Check)

Our backend uses Firebase App Check with Apple App Attest to verify that analysis requests originate from a genuine, unmodified copy of Gainly. The attestation token does not identify you personally.

7. Apple Health Integration

Pro users can optionally enable Apple Health sync in Profile → Preferences. When enabled, Gainly writes the following data to the Health app on your device:

  • Completed workout sessions (duration, type, estimated calories)
  • Body weight entries
  • Water intake logs

This integration is entirely opt-in. You can disable it at any time in Preferences. No Health data is read or transmitted to our servers — writes go directly from Gainly to the Health app on your device.

8. Subscriptions and Payments

Gainly offers two optional paid subscription tiers, both processed exclusively through Apple In-App Purchases:

  • Pro Lite — unlocks non-AI advanced features (workout library, advanced charts, Apple Health sync, and more). Does not include AI-powered analysis.
  • Pro — includes everything in Pro Lite plus all AI features (physique analysis, AI training plans, nutrition scanner, and grocery planner).

We never see or store your payment card details. Subscription management — including cancellation — is handled through your Apple ID settings. Purchase receipts are validated through Apple's servers.

9. Notifications

If you grant notification permission, Gainly may send daily workout reminders, inactivity nudges, streak alerts, and milestone celebrations. You can disable notifications at any time in iOS Settings → Notifications → Gainly or from Profile → Preferences inside the app.

10. Children's Privacy

Gainly is intended for users aged 18 and older. The AI physique analysis feature is designed for adults and includes programmatic safeguards against analysing photos of minors. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 18. If you believe a minor has used the app, please contact us at turlybekdostan@gmail.com.

11. Data Retention and Deletion

  • All data on your device is deleted when you delete the app.
  • iCloud data can be removed via iOS Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage.
  • To request deletion of server-side data (anonymous UUID + analysis counters in Firestore), email turlybekdostan@gmail.com. We will process your request within 30 days.

12. Your Rights

  • Access — all data Gainly holds about you is visible within the app itself
  • Deletion — delete the app to remove all local data; email us to remove server-side data
  • Opt-out — disable notifications, iCloud sync, or Apple Health sync at any time
  • Questions — contact us at turlybekdostan@gmail.com

13. Changes to This Policy
